What is Society for Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE)?

Leading Hispanics in STEM

SHPE is the nation’s largest association dedicated to fostering Hispanic leadership in the STEM field.

Chapter History

Mission

SHPE changes lives by empowering the Hispanic community to realize its fullest potential and to impact the world through STEM awareness, access, support, and development.

Vision

SHPE’s vision is a world where Hispanics are highly valued and influential as the leading innovators, scientists, mathematicians, and engineers.

Our Purpose

To promote Professional Development among our members in the fields of Science, Technology, Engineering & Mathematics (STEM)

To reveal members' full potential and to provide opportunities to develop their leadership skills

To achieve Academic Excellence and Retention among members

To unify and sustain the chapter through recruitment, retainment, fundraising & networking

To inspire Hispanic youth to pursue higher education through awareness, access & support in STEM

To reciprocate the abundant support from the scholastic, local & Hispanic communities

Officer Committees

Click the symbol above each committee to join!
Webmaster

The Webmaster Committee is an essential part of the Sac SHPE chapter as so many individuals utilize the web services to obtain current information about events, resources for information about scholarships and current officer and chapter information, and other opportunities. The website includes a webpage to sign up and join the Sac SHPE chapter which needs to be maintained and encourage students to become members. We provide easy and quick access to joining our chapter as well as for information that our members and the Sac State community can access. We will be working on the SacSHPE.org website and help committee members become better web developers and leaders. A student of any major is encouraged to join.


Junior Chapter Representative

The Jr Chapter Committee focuses on having members work with the local Jr Chapter. Our Jr Chapter is full of students that are interested in the engineering field. Some of these students might not have a great understanding on what the different fields are and how they differ from each other. The Jr Chapter Events are meant to help these students find that out. Members of the committee can also give these students personal input on college, what their major is like, how they chose their major, and other similar questions the students have. It would be ideal for the Jr Chapter committee to have a diverse list of majors so that the events can be planned accordingly to focus on a specific type of engineering.
